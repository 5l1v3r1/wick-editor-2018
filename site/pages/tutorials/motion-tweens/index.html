Using Motion Tweens

Tutorial by zrispo

...

Motion tweens are useful! You can use them to smoothly animate objects from one place to another.

Pictured: An example of an animation using motion tweens.

Let's make our first motion tween! Start by drawing something on the first frame of your project. Now, drag the right edge of the frame to make it longer.

Pictured: Dragging a frame to make it longer

Now let's make a tween! Click on the beginning of the long frame and then click the "Create Motion Tween" button in the Inspector.

The "Create Motion Tween" button in the inspector.

The frame has a little dot now. This is the first ... Now, do the same at the end of the frame.

Pictured: A frame with one keyframe.

Now you should have two dots on the frame - these are the keyframes of the tween.

Pictured: A frame with two keyframes

Each keyframe represents a position, rotation, or scale of the object being tweened. Click on the second keyframe and move the object somewhere else and then press the play button. You've just created a motion tween from one place to another!

Pictured: A simple motion tween.

Try adding more keyframes or rotating/scaling/changing the objects opacity to create some nice effects!

Pictured: A more complicated motion tween.

Tips

Easing Direction

"Easing" is ... there are four options: None, In, Out, and In-Out.

Pictured: From left to right: No direction, In, Out, In-Out.

You can select which easing direction your tween uses by clicking the "Easing" dropdown menu in the Inspector.
